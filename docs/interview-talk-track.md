# Interview Talk Track

## How to Explain This Project in an Interview

### 1) What was the goal of the project?
I wanted to document how I would investigate QRadar offenses in a way that reflects actual SOC reasoning rather than just screenshot collection.

### 2) What was the most valuable finding?
The most valuable pattern was not just repeated failures — it was the combination of:
- broad username targeting,
- repeated source concentration,
- privileged account focus,
- and fail-to-success behavior.

### 3) What makes this project practical?
Each offense writeup focuses on:
- what triggered,
- why it matters,
- what evidence supports it,
- and what I would validate next in a real environment.

### 4) What did you learn from this?
That offense investigation is strongest when alerts are not treated in isolation. The real value comes from pivoting, grouping behavior, and documenting conclusions clearly.
